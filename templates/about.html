This website was inpired by Miguel Medeiros's satoshisperperson.com. I decided to make a site like his for the Nano cryptocurrency. Nano is a digital currency that utilizes a block-lattice structure (where every account has its own blockchain) and a consensus mechanism called Open Representative Voting to enable near-instant, extremely energy-efficient, and completely feeless transactions. You can learn more about Nano on it's official website and at nano.community.

While a single Bitcoin can be divided to just 8 decimal places, Nano can be divided down to 30. The smallest unit of a Nano is called "raw". There's 1 nonillion (short scale) or 1 quintillion (long scale) raw in a single Nano. This extreme divisibility means that there's an absurd amount of raw per person in the world. The total supply of Nano is about 133 million. Even if it was reduced to just 1 Nano, there would still be 125,000,000,000,000,000,000 raw per person, assuming a population of 8 billion.

Some people might be wondering why the Available Nano Supply section on the home page says 133,246,492 instead of the oft-repeated 133,248,297. The latter value is what the available supply was after the leftover funds were burnt back in 2017, following the shutdown of the original faucet. However, random people have sent additional Nano to the burn address since then, slightly lowering the amount of Nano in circulation. This website uses the "available_supply" RPC command to get the current circulating supply.

The live population counter is based on this website. Of course, it only shows a very rough estimate based on data from the U.N. and other international organizations. It's impossible to know what Earth's actual population is at any given time.
